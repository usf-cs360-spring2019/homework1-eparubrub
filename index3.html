Introduction

The following data visualizations are from the SFPD Incidents dataset for the month of December 2018. All visualizations include a screenshot of a tableau worksheet that I created in the top square. In the bottom square, there is a d3 implementation of the tableau visualization.

Visualization 1

Interpretation

This visualization uses a line chart in order to analyze the frequency of police incident reports by the hour of day.

Discussion

I chose to visualize this because I thought it would be interesting to see what times of the day might have more frequency of incidents. As we can see, there is a very large dip in occurences between the hours of 12AM and 8AM while incidents peak at around 12PM and 6PM.

Visualization 2

Interpretation

This visualization uses a bar chart in order to analyze the frequency of police incident reports according to the top 10 most frequently reported categories.

Discussion

I chose to visualize the top categories of incidents that were reported because I thought it would be interesting to see what types of incidents happened more frequently than others. As we can see, larcency theft occured much more frequently than the other categories listed.

Visualization 3

Interpretation

This visualization uses a stacked bar chart in order to analayze the frequency of specific categories of incident reports within the Tenderloin. These categories are the top 5 most frequently reported categories in the Tenderloin neighborhood.

Discussion

This visualization was made because I was curious to know the different categories of incidents that happened in the area with the most incidents. According to the data, I found that the neighborhood with the most reported incidents was the tenderloin. From there, I went to see the top categories of incidents and came up with the idea of using a stacked bar in order to compare the occurences of the categories. We can see that larcency theft dominates most of the graph but other categories can take a considerable chunk as well.

Overview

Overall, it was very interesting to be able to use the data from the SFPD and visualize the different aspects of incidents that occur throughout San Francisco.
